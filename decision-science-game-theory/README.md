# Decision Science & Game Theory

> **The promise:** By Day 65, you can dissect any hard decision or AI product design into its decision-theoretic, game-theoretic, and behavioral layers — and act on the analysis.

---

## Who This Course Is For

Practitioners and builders (L1/L2) comfortable with math and statistics who think in systems. The two outcomes this course optimises for: (1) designing AI products and features grounded in decision and incentive theory, and (2) making better career and life decisions with formal tools. No prior economics or game theory required. Intellectual patience is.

This course is *not* for you if you want a fast survey without the "so what," or if you are allergic to occasionally working through an equation.

---

## The Arc at a Glance

Days 1–13 build the foundation of *individual* decision-making. You start by confronting an uncomfortable truth — your brain runs two competing systems and you have limited control over which one drives. From there you construct the rational benchmark (expected utility), then systematically map how and why real humans deviate from it (cognitive biases, Prospect Theory). By Day 13 you have a complete personal decision toolkit: structure choices, assign probabilities, update on evidence, value information, and recognise when your own cognition is the obstacle.

Days 14–40 expand the world from one player to many. The moment a second rational agent enters the room, the entire landscape changes — the right choice is no longer calculable in isolation. Nash equilibrium, repeated games, mechanism design, and the Shapley value emerge as tools for reasoning under strategic interaction. Days 41–55 translate everything into AI product craft: reinforcement learning as sequential decision-making, reward design as mechanism specification, alignment as the hardest mechanism design problem in existence. Days 56–65 land the theory back in your own career and life, ending with a capstone that forces you to apply everything at once.

## How to Use This Course

- **Rhythm:** One page per day, 30–45 min of focused reading.
- **Exercises:** Required for L2 depth. Attempt before checking hints or solutions.
- **Bibliography:** Open [`bibliography.md`](bibliography.md) when a page cites a primary source. Do not read ahead.
- **Rest & Synthesise days:** Do the consolidation prompt in writing — re-reading alone does not stick.
- **If life intervenes:** Redo yesterday's page before starting today's. Never skip rest days.

## The Capstone (Day 65)

Choose one AI feature you are building (or could build) and one major personal decision you are currently facing. Apply the unified framework: map what is uncertain, who else is choosing strategically, and which cognitive biases are active. Write a 2-page decision memo for each. *Done* means a colleague unfamiliar with this course reads your memo and learns something non-obvious.
