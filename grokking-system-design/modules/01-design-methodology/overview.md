# Module 1 — The Design Methodology

**Days 1–3 · 3 pages · ~1.5 hours**

---

## What This Module Earns You

Before this module, you approach a system design problem by instinct: you draw the boxes you've seen before, pick the database you know best, and hope the architecture holds under load. After this module, you have three instruments that every subsequent day in this course depends on:

1. **A methodology** (Day 1) — a four-step process for decomposing any design problem into functional requirements, non-functional requirements, capacity estimates, and a data flow sketch. The methodology is the difference between a blank page and a starting point.

2. **A decision framework** (Day 2) — a repeatable process for choosing between competing design options by scoring them against the quality attributes that matter most *for this specific system*. Every building block day in Modules 2–3 includes a Decision Guide section that applies this framework. Every design day in Module 5 is structured around it.

3. **A visual grammar** (Day 3) — the C4 model, a four-level zoom system that lets you draw any system at exactly the right level of detail for any audience. Every HLD and LLD you produce in this course uses this grammar.

---

## What Is Not Covered Here

This module teaches instruments, not building blocks. You will not encounter databases, caches, load balancers, or message queues until Module 2. The temptation to jump ahead is understandable — resist it. Learners who skip Day 2 in particular consistently make the same mistake in Module 5: they know the building blocks but cannot justify their selections under questioning.
