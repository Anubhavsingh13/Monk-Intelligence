# Module 1 — Perception (Days 1–8)

> **What this module earns you:** By the end of Day 8, you will have a complete shift in how imperfection, space, and impermanence *feel*. The world will not have changed. Your relationship with it will have.

---

## What This Module Is For

Before you can use Japanese philosophy, you have to see with it.

Most people encounter wabi-sabi and ikigai as isolated concepts — a design aesthetic, a productivity framework. What makes them powerful is not each concept individually but the *underlying stance* they all share: a relationship with reality that moves *with* the grain of things rather than against it.

This module builds that stance from the ground up. It does not ask you to change your behaviour. It asks you to change what you notice.

The eight days move from the broadest (the whole Japanese worldview) to the most intimate (the quality of your own attention):

- **Days 1–2** establish the lens: what Japanese philosophy is and the instrument — kokoro, the heart-mind — through which it is received.
- **Days 3–4** work on your relationship with imperfection and impermanence: mono no aware and wabi-sabi.
- **Days 5–6** work on your relationship with space and mystery: ma and yūgen.
- **Days 7–8** bring the perception arc to its close: ichi-go ichi-e (presence as recognition) and shoshin (openness as the highest skill).

---

## The Central Tension This Module Resolves

Most of us live with a background hum of dissatisfaction — a sense that things should be slightly different, more polished, more resolved. This is not a character flaw. It is a deeply ingrained philosophical habit: the idea that the present moment is a draft, and the good version is coming.

Japanese philosophy's answer is not to lower your standards. It is to question the premise — to ask whether what you're calling a draft might actually be the finished thing, seen properly.

By Day 8, you will have encountered this question eight times from eight different angles. That is how a new way of seeing takes hold: not through a single insight but through patient, repeated contact from different directions.

## Before You Move to Module 2

After Day 8, take a few minutes before beginning the Rest & Synthesize day to write a single paragraph: *How has the way I see one specific part of my daily life shifted over these eight days?* It does not need to be dramatic. Small shifts are the point. This paragraph will become useful when you reach the capstone on Day 21.
